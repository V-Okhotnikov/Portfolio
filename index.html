Asset Integrity Management (AIM)

A systematic, holistic approach to ensure that assets are designed, operated, and maintained to perform their required function effectively and safely throughout their lifecycle.

Corrosion Loops

Diagrams that group process equipment with similar corrosion mechanisms. Used to streamline inspection planning and monitoring. Image: A schematic diagram showing piping and equipment color-coded by corrosion mechanism.

Failure Modes

Identification and analysis of the ways in which equipment can fail (e.g., thinning, cracking, fatigue). The foundation of Risk-Based Inspection (RBI). Image: A chart listing common failure modes for pressure equipment with icons.

Integrity Operating Windows (IOWs)

Established limits for process variables (like pressure, temperature, chemistry) that must be controlled to maintain asset integrity. Image: A dashboard screenshot showing process parameters with green/yellow/red operational limits.

Risk Based Inspection (RBI)

A methodology that uses risk as a basis for prioritizing and managing inspection programs, focusing resources on the highest-risk assets. Image: A risk matrix plot showing equipment items ranked by probability and consequence of failure.

Manage Threats and Opportunities

Proactive processes to identify and mitigate potential threats to integrity while capitalizing on opportunities to improve performance and extend life. Image: A flowchart illustrating the threat management process from identification to resolution.

Management of Change (MOC)

A formal system to review and approve any changes to equipment, processes, or materials to ensure no new risks are introduced. Image: A document or form template for a Management of Change request.

Permit to Work System

A core safety procedure to control high-risk work activities, ensuring all hazards are identified and precautions are in place before work begins. Image: A photo of an engineer reviewing a paper-based or digital permit at a job site.

Asset Integrity and Process Safety

The direct link between mechanical integrity of equipment and the prevention of catastrophic process safety events. Image: An overlapping Venn diagram showing Asset Integrity, Process Safety, and Operational Excellence.

QA/QC

Quality Assurance and Quality Control processes applied during fabrication, repair, and installation to ensure materials and workmanship meet specifications. Image: A welder being inspected by a QC inspector using measurement tools.

Maintenance and Integrity Execution

The practical planning and execution of inspection, maintenance, and repair activities to sustain asset reliability and availability. Image: A team of technicians executing a planned maintenance task on a large pump or valve.

Static Equipment Inspection

Detailed inspection methodologies and best practices for non-rotating plant equipment to ensure their continued safe and reliable operation.

Pressure Vessels Inspection

Internal and external inspection of drums, columns, and reactors for corrosion, erosion, and cracking. Utilizes NDT methods like UT thickness testing and MT/PT. Image: An inspector performing an internal examination of a large vessel with a flashlight.

Flare System Inspection

Inspection of flare tips, stacks, knock-out drums, and piping for thermal degradation, corrosion, and structural integrity. Often requires specialized access. Image: A drone view of a tall flare stack, with an inset of a corroded flare tip.

Heat Exchangers Inspection

Tube bundle pulling, tube inspection (ECT, IRIS), shell and channel examination. Critical for maintaining heat transfer efficiency and preventing leaks. Image: A pulled tube bundle laid out on racks, with an inspector checking tube ends.

Boilers and WHRU Inspection

Inspection of furnaces, tubes, refractory, and headers in boilers and Waste Heat Recovery Units. Focus on high-temperature damage mechanisms. Image: An internal view of a boiler showing tube banks and refractory lining.

Piping Inspection

Circuitization and baseline inspection of process piping, focusing on corrosion under insulation (CUI), erosion-corrosion, and fatigue. Image: An inspector using an ultrasonic thickness gauge on a pipe, possibly with insulation removed.

Tanks Inspection

Above-ground storage tank (AST) inspection including floor scanning, shell courses, roof, and foundation assessment per API 653. Image: An inspector on a raft performing ultrasonic testing on a tank floor.

Valves Inspections

In-service and bench inspection of block valves, control valves, and relief devices (PSVs) for proper operation, leakage, and internal damage. Image: A maintenance technician testing a safety relief valve on a calibration bench.

Digital Transformation in Inspection

Embracing cutting-edge technologies to make inspection processes smarter, safer, more efficient, and more predictive.

AI for Inspection

Machine learning algorithms analyze vast datasets (images, sensor data) to automatically detect anomalies, predict corrosion rates, and prioritize findings. Image: A software interface showing an AI-highlighted indication on a radiograph or corrosion map.

Inspection using AR

Augmented Reality glasses overlay inspection history, P&IDs, and thickness data onto the engineer's field of view, providing real-time contextual information. Image: An engineer wearing AR glasses looking at a vessel, with digital data overlaid in the image.

Robotic and Drone Inspection

Deploying crawlers, drones, and submersibles for access-confined or hazardous areas. Captures high-resolution visuals and sensor data without human entry. Image: A drone flying near a tall column or a crawler robot inside a confined space.

Advanced NDT and Digital Integration

Phased Array UT, Digital Radiography (DR), and Laser Scanning produce rich digital data that integrates directly with Digital Twin models. Image: A screen showing a 3D point cloud model of a pipe rack generated from a laser scan.

IoT and Continuous Monitoring

Permanently installed wireless sensors (UT, AE, corrosion probes) provide real-time integrity data, enabling a shift from periodic to continuous monitoring. Image: A diagram showing wireless sensors on equipment transmitting data to a cloud-based dashboard.

AI-Driven and Dynamic RBI

RBI programs that update risk rankings in real-time based on live operational data, inspection results, and AI-predicted degradation. Image: A dynamic dashboard where asset risk scores change color based on real-time feed from process and integrity data.
