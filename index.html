General Inspection and Maintenance Processes

  1. Visual Inspections (VT):
    • Conducted on all equipment to identify surface defects, corrosion, and mechanical damage.
  2. Non-Destructive Testing (NDT):
    • Ultrasonic Testing (UT): Used to measure wall thickness and detect internal flaws.
    • Magnetic Particle Testing (MT): Applied to detect surface and near-surface cracks.
    • Radiographic Testing (RT): Used for internal defect detection in welds and components.
  3. Borescope Inspections:
    • Performed to inspect internal components of vessels, heat exchangers, tanks, and rotating equipment.
  4. Hydrostatic and Pneumatic Testing:
    • Conducted to verify the integrity of vessels, heat exchangers, tanks, piping system, and valves.
  5. Composite Repairs:
    • Applied to repair localized corrosion or damage on vessels, tanks, and piping system.
  6. Welding Repairs:
    • Performed to address cracks, corrosion, or mechanical damage in vessels, piping system, and rotating equipment.
  7. Corrosion Under Insulation (CUI) Monitoring:
    • Conducted on vessels and piping systems to detect hidden corrosion.
  8. Insulation Replacement:
    • Replacing GRP insulation with metal or foam glass on vessels, tanks, and piping systems
  9. Leak Detection:
    • Using IR cameras and acoustic methods to identify leaks in piping systems and rotating equipment.

Equipment-Specific Processes

Vessels

  1. Internal Component Inspections:
    • Cyclones, swirlers, and other internals inspected for wear, corrosion, and blockages.
  2. Flange Testing:
    • Hydrostatic testing of flanges to ensure sealing integrity.
  3. Internal Coating Restoration:
    • Restoring protective coatings in vessels.

Heat Exchangers

  1. Tube Bundle Cleaning:
    • Removing fouling and deposits from tube bundles.
  2. Tube Plugging:
    • Plugging leaking or damaged tubes to maintain performance.
  3. Preservation:
    • Applying preservation methods during shutdowns.

Tanks

  1. Geodetic Measurements:
    • Monitoring tank settlement and deformation.
  2. Robotic Inspections:
    • Using Stingray robots for tank bottom inspections.
  3. Advanced NDT Methods:
    • Higher Order Modes Cluster (HOMC) and Pulsed Eddy Current (PEC) for corrosion mapping.
  4. Acoustic Emission Testing (TankPAC):
    • Detecting active corrosion and leaks.
  5. Internal Coating Restoration:
    • Restoring coatings in tanks.

Piping System

  1. Erosion Monitoring:
    • Inspecting for erosion in high-velocity sections.
  2. Dead Legs Inspections:
    • Checking for corrosion in low-flow or stagnant areas.

Flare System

  1. UAV Inspections:
    • Using drones to inspect flare tips and structural integrity.
  2. Flare Tip Replacement:
    • Replacing damaged or worn flare tips.

Rotating Equipment

  1. Root Cause Analysis (RCA):
    • Investigating failures in pumps, compressors, and motors.
  2. Motor Inspections:
    • Inspecting motor windings, bearings, and alignment.

Valves and Pressure Relief Devices (PRDs)

  1. Functional Testing:
    • Testing valves and PRDs for proper operation.
  2. Rupture Disc Replacement:
    • Replacing worn or damaged rupture discs.
